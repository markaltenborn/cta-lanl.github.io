OPPORTUNITIES

CTA hires across every research-career stage — from staff scientists down to high-school summer researchers. Many CTA staff began their LANL careers as postdocs and grew into permanent positions; the postdoc → staff path is one of the most travelled routes into the lab.

CTA postdocs work on theoretical and computational astrophysics across an active portfolio of LANL projects. The center is open to working with any LANL postdoc whose research overlaps with our group — including postdocs hired on a job ad outside CTA. Shared projects can make you a CTA member regardless of which line of funding hired you.

Application and eligibility

Eligibility, salary, benefits, and the formal application process all match the standard LANL postdoc requirements. The LANL postdoc page has the current details and the official application portal.

CTA also runs its own seasonal postdoc call posted to the LANL postdoc portal. When the call is open, the CTA postdoc ad → is the most direct way in. See Important dates below for the cycle.

Appointment types

Research Associate

The standard LANL postdoc appointment. Funding comes from a specific program or from Laboratory Directed Research and Development (LDRD). Most CTA postdocs are hired on Research Associate appointments tied to a CTA project.

Named Fellowships

Two-year, fully funded fellowships sponsored by individual LANL programs. Several have direct overlap with CTA research:

Harold Agnew National Security Fellow

Funded by the LANL Weapons Program. Open to research aligned with the lab’s national-security mission across experimental, theoretical, computational, and engineering work. Two-year, fully funded.

Mark Kac Fellow in Applied Mathematics

Up to one fellow per year. Jointly sponsored by DOE ASCR and the LANL Center for Nonlinear Studies. Aimed at DOE-relevant applied math.

Chick Keller Fellow in Space and Earth Science

Hosted by the Center for Space and Earth Science, which spans astrophysics, heliophysics, geophysics, planetary science, earth systems, and bioscience. Provides half-time funding.

Nicholas C. Metropolis Fellow

Funded by the Advanced Simulation Computing (ASC) program. Computational physics and computer science emphasis with access to LANL’s largest supercomputers. Two-year, fully funded.

Distinguished Fellowships

The most competitive postdoc fellowships at LANL, awarded to candidates with extraordinary research records and clear potential to lead future programs. Candidates must be nominated and sponsored by a LANL technical-staff member; sponsored packages are reviewed at the Fall Quarterly Review. CTA staff are happy to discuss sponsorship for strong matches — reach out to a CTA scientist whose work overlaps with yours before applying.

Richard P. Feynman Fellow

Theory and computing emphasis.

Darlene Christian Hoffman Fellow

Recognizes outstanding contributions to science and engineering by women.

J. Robert Oppenheimer (JRO) Fellow

Research aligned with LANL’s mission.

Frederick Reines (FR) Fellow

Experimental sciences emphasis.

Director’s Fellowships

Director’s Fellows have considerable freedom to pursue independent research while building ties to LANL programs through part-time programmatic work — designed as a long-term pipeline into permanent Lab roles. Like the Distinguished Fellowships, sponsorship by a LANL technical-staff member is required; sponsored packages are reviewed quarterly.

Postdoc → staff track record

Many CTA staff scientists started as CTA postdocs and converted to permanent LANL roles through the standard conversion process. The postdoc → staff route remains the most travelled path into the lab.

CTA postdocs who do not convert have placed strongly elsewhere — faculty positions at research universities, scientist roles at other US national laboratories, and senior research positions in industry.

Graduate students join CTA via LANL’s Graduate Research Assistant program. Most appointments are summer (10–12 weeks) but longer-term appointments are also possible for graduate students.

The ideal time to apply for a summer appointment is November or December of the preceding year. LANL student programs →

CTA hosts undergraduate summer researchers each year, typically matched to a specific project and CTA mentor. CTA can typically support roughly 20–30 students each summer across all student levels.

Apply November or December for the following summer via the LANL Student Programs Office. CTA also leverages LANL summer schools in computational physics and astronomy as additional paths in. These programs are run by LANL, not CTA — the catalogue and dates change year to year, so check the LANL pages for current openings.

CTA does not run a dedicated high-school program. Instead, CTA mentors host high-school researchers through LANL’s Student Programs Office, which offers opportunities such as SAGE and the LANL High School Internship Program. These are LANL programs — not CTA-specific — but CTA scientists regularly take on student researchers from them.

Placement depends on which CTA members are offering projects in a given year. If you are a high-school student interested in CTA research, apply through the Student Programs Office and reach out to a potential mentor — see our staff and postdoc pages.
