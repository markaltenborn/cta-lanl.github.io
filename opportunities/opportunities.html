Most CTA staff scientists begin their LANL careers as postdocs and convert to permanent positions through the standard LANL conversion process. External staff hires also happen but are less common.

If you are interested in joining CTA as staff, the most reliable route is to start as a postdoc, build a strong research record, and convert. Questions about the path? Wes Even (CTA Director).

CTA postdocs work on theoretical and computational astrophysics across an active portfolio of LANL projects. There are three pathways into a CTA postdoc role:

1. Standard project postdoc

When a CTA project is hiring, the project advertises a specific postdoc role tied to that work. Apply directly to the project ad. This is the most common entry path. Open project postings (when available) appear on the LANL postdoc portal.

2. Seasonal CTA call

Once a year the CTA puts out a center-wide postdoc call. Apply once; matching to active projects happens after review. Successful applicants may be funded as standard project postdocs or via a named LANL fellowship (see below).

3. Named LANL fellowships

Highly competitive lab-wide fellowships. CTA-relevant programs include:

Director’s Postdoctoral Fellowship

General LANL fellowship for distinguished candidates across all science divisions. Open to many CTA-relevant areas.

Distinguished Oppenheimer Fellowship

LANL’s most prestigious postdoc fellowship. Awarded to a small number of exceptional candidates each year.

Metropolis Postdoctoral Fellowship in Computational Physics

Computational-physics emphasis. High overlap with CTA’s modeling and simulation work.

Other LANL fellowships exist (Feynman, Kac, Reines, Agnew, and more). Most are open to CTA-relevant candidates. See the full list of LANL postdoc fellowships →

Graduate students join CTA via LANL’s Graduate Research Assistant program. Most appointments are summer (10–12 weeks) but longer-term appointments — up to two years — are also possible for graduate students.

The ideal time to apply for a summer appointment is November or December of the preceding year. LANL student programs →

Post-master’s appointments are full-time positions at LANL for up to two years, intended for candidates who hold a master’s degree and are considering further graduate study or transition to research roles.
